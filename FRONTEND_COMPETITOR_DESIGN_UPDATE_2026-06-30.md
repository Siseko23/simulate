# FreightFlow Nexus Frontend Competitor Design Update — 2026-06-30

## Benchmark reviewed
Linebooker positions itself around scale, enterprise transport management, large transporter network access, procurement, operations and payment management.

## Frontend changes implemented
- Rebuilt the public landing page into a stronger product-style homepage.
- Added sharper hero copy: "Book a truck in minutes, not days".
- Added product mockup card showing quote, label, PO, dispatch and POD milestones.
- Added four trust metrics: self-serve start, visible fee, supplier SLA and QR proof.
- Added platform cards for shipper, supplier, driver, Kargo AI, labels/waybills and payment flow.
- Added workflow section: shipment → quote → label → supplier PO → dispatch → QR POD.
- Added competitor-aware positioning without directly attacking Linebooker.
- Added FreightFlow AI / Kargo section to brand the AI as FreightFlow-owned.
- Improved responsive styling for desktop and mobile.

## Strategic positioning
FreightFlow should not claim to have a larger network than established enterprise freight platforms. The front end now positions FreightFlow as:
- faster to start,
- more transparent,
- more self-serve,
- better for SMEs and owner-operated suppliers,
- more visible around labels, waybills, QR scan, POD and payout flow.
